Results

Accuracy

After we selected our model, we ran it against the actual FIFA World Cup 2018 games to compute the accuracy our model would have had on predicting games of the competitions. We've reached the following accuracy:

61.9%

Our model is pretty weak at predicting draws, and this past World Cup had a lot of them. Also, soccer is not an easy sport to predict which makes it a beautiful sport. We were not able to come up with great feature to segregate wins from loss or draw.

Simulation

We used our model that predict the outcome of a game to simulate the whole World Cup competition. A simulation starts with the initial groups defined by the FIFA draw and simulates each game of the group phase in order to determine which teams will access the knockout phase. Then, we follow the rules of the FIFA World Cup to determine which team plays against which team in the knockout phase, and we simulate the games until the final. Data

Scrapping

Once we decided what question we wanted to answer (e.g. what is the probability for the home team to beat the away team), we needed data. We were provided with a database of a lot of international games. We decided to augment this dataset with data about the home and away teams at this point. The video games FIFA has been around for quite some times and they've been rating players and teams for quite some times. We decided it would be smart to grab information about the teams at the time of the games based on how the video game FIFA grade them. We wrote a scrapper for SoFifa (http://sofifa.com). It would grab all the stats for each national team available on each version of FIFA (e.g FIFA06, FIFA10, ...) as well as the stats of each of the player composing the team. The code of the scrapper is available here.

Feature Engineering

We used all this scrapped data to create feature such as the difference of average age for the attackers between the home and away team, or the difference of average speed between the home attack and the away defense. This was done by aggregating the stats about the player of each team for each game of the initial dataset and when it happened.
